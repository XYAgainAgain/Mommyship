# Ecology of Osminok Prime

Every creature in this section has two names. The **shortname** is a two-component compound roughly two phonemes long, where the first half describes a defining trait and the second half says what the thing *is*.

Recurring roots mark recurring features: "meat" is anything eaten for protein, "crunch" is a hard shell or exoskeleton, "storm" tags something associated with surface weather. The full Osminoki name (in italics after the shortname) is a literal, often comically specific translated description that reads more like a field note than a taxonomic label.

What follows is a representative trophic sample, not an exhaustive catalog. Osminok's ocean supports untold millions of species across nine major biome types; these entries cover the organisms most relevant to Osminoki survival, culture, and daily life. The real ecosystem is orders of magnitude more complex and gets stranger the closer one looks.

> **Key Biological Absences:** Osminok has no mammals, no fungi, and no avian life. Convergent evolution produces mammal-like traits (endothermy in Shallowskates and Fastfriends, parental investment in Nursemouths), but Osminok's entirely aquatic evolution never produced a synapsid-like lineage. Nothing on Osminok has feathers, and the planet's negligible landmass means there is no safe nesting ground for any non-amphibious flier; the closest thing to aerial locomotion is the Stormskate's storm-surface leaping, which is less "flight" and more "being launched."
>
> All decomposition is bacterial, archaeal, or crystalline, and much slower for it; carcasses persist for decades, which is why Bone Yards function as long-term food sources. The total absence of fungal life means Osminoki evolved with zero resistance to fungal pathogens. Off-world, this becomes a serious problem: constant skin infections, respiratory issues, gill irritation, and a significant chunk of earnings spent on anti-fungal treatments. Space-faring Osminoki gravitate toward cold ship sections where fungi struggle and they feel at home.

## Ocean Biomes

**1. The Twilight Shelves (200–1,000m)**
Sloping continental shelves where faint blue light still penetrates during calm periods. The richest biome on the planet: Kelp Forests anchor the substrate, Smallplant blooms drive the diel migration through the water column, and the resulting food chain supports everything from Meatmeat clouds to Sleekfish pods. Stingplant gardens dot the rocky outcrops, Crunchcleaner stations occupy prominent rocks, and Kelpgrazers drift through the fronds cropping new growth. Home to most Osminoki, who build Glowspiral-lit living spaces in the Kelp Forest canopy with Fastfriends patrolling nearby. Where most adults live and most meals happen. Temperature: 2–4°C. Moderate to intense bioluminescence.

**2. The Midnight Plains (1,000–3,000m)**
Vast expanses of fine sediment where Siftslug trails create the only navigable paths through murky water and Grubmeat burrows through the substrate below. Trench-Crawlers scavenge in enormous numbers, Flatmeat hide under the sediment, and Sleekfish pods cruise the open water hunting whatever moves. Too open, too flat, too visible; ambush predators bury themselves here and count on the lack of cover. Osminoki cross the Plains when they must but do not linger. Temperature: 0–2°C. Sparse bioluminescence (anything glowing here is either bait or a mistake).

**3. The Vent Gardens (2,000–4,000m, scattered)**
Hydrothermal vent fields scattered across the deep ocean floor. Ventplant mats coat the surrounding rock, supporting dense colonies of Frilltube, Ventshell, and Crunchsnack. Stingplant gardens cluster at the warm margins, their killing arms sheltering the Stingfish whose eggs are a prized snack. Clingtubes rise up to ten meters from the vent-rock, and Crunchcleaner stations thrive in the mineral-rich water. Osminoki use the Gardens for warmth, mineral harvesting, social gathering, egg-laying, and eating (everything here tastes faintly of sulfur, but some things are worth it). Wild geyser eruptions are the primary hazard. Temperature: 0°C ambient, 400°C at vents. Intense bioluminescence.

**4. The Crushing Deep (4,000–10,000m)**
Steep-walled trenches and abyssal canyons where Voidcoral reefs grow on every surface and Blacksludge patches sit undisturbed for millennia. Where the Starsingers evolved. Where the Teuthidan Abbey is located (~7,000m, carved into trench wall basalt). Home to the largest predators: Darkmaw lures glow in the darkness, Crushing Coils lie silent against the walls, and Screamers patrol the open canyons. Pressure Mites infest anything that lingers too long. Only the most adapted survive here. Temperature: variable but always frigid. Rare, ominous bioluminescence.

**5. The Bone Yards (variable depth, scattered)**
Massive carcasses of dead megafauna supporting entire ecosystems for generations. Each carcass is a temporary world: Trench-Crawlers strip the soft tissue, Bonebloom colonies grow gardens of colored crystal glass from the bones, and Voidcoral slowly incorporates everything into permanent reef structure. Dangerous scavenging grounds (Bloodswarms and worse are always nearby), but also the primary source of bone tools, shell material, and the metals locked in ancient Voidcoral formations. Extreme bioluminescence.

**6. The Ice Shelves (polar regions, 0–500m)**
Undersides of massive ice formations hosting rich, concentrated ecosystems fed by some of the most nutrient-dense water on the planet. The sea floor is shallower here, packing life into tight layers; temperatures are brutal but rock-stable; and the cold means everything grows larger and fattier. Polar-dwelling Osminoki are hardier than their deep-water kin and actually have the *least* difficulty scraping out a living. The real danger is the surface: wind above the ice is too fast and cold for rain, so any Osminoki caught topside dries out with deadly speed. Below the ice, though, the hunting is excellent, especially with polar Fastfriend variants, which run heavier and meaner than their temperate cousins. Glitterscales drift in the frigid shallows, and camouflaged predators unique to this biome lurk among the frozen formations.

**7. The Living Trenches (7,000m+)**
Where the largest, oldest Singers reside. Off-limits except to Abbey initiates for environmental and personal safety. Geological activity is constant: landslides, black smokers, brine pools. The Singers' own glow and EM fields create a truly indescribable otherworldly atmosphere.

**8. The Forgotten Depths (12,000–20,000m)**
Mostly unexplored. For Osminoki, the pressure only becomes slightly uncomfortable here. The Abbey has sent expeditions, but few returned. These depths contain ancient Singers that never ascended, extremophile organisms unlike anything in shallower zones, and geological features that probably shouldn't exist. Possibly Light Eater territory.

**9. The Unknowable Deep (20,000m+)**
May not exist, or may be completely inaccessible. The absence of continental crust and the planet's high core density allow steeper geometry than most planets' tectonic systems support. Legends speak of trenches that "go down forever." The Abbey's deepest listening posts occasionally detect low-frequency rumbles, anomalous EM pulses, and pressure waves from below, but expeditions find nothing. It's still worth a look, though! Bring snacks and/or become one.

**Timekeeping (The Living Clock):** The Osminoki don't track the sun; they can scarcely see it. They track the "Great Rising and Falling" thusly:

1. Smallplants rise toward faint surface light during local solar noon.
2. Smallmeat follows.
3. Meatmeat swarms after the Smallmeat.
4. Predators follow the Meatmeat.

This creates a density wave through the water column. At "night," everything reverses. Osminoki sense this through pressure changes, bioluminescence shifts, chemical signatures, and EM field fluctuations. The zone where this migration is most visible (~50–500m) is called the **Shimmerlayer**.

## What We Eat

The Osminoki categorize food organisms primarily by reliability and desirability, not by biological classification.

#### Smallplant

*"The Smallest Ones Who Rise Toward Light And Upon Whom All Living Things Depend"*

| **Type:** *Flora* | **Role:** *Producer* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones (concentrated in Twilight Shelves)* | **Size:** *Microscopic* | **Analog:** *Phytoplankton* |

The invisible foundation. Trillions of photosynthetic microorganisms that rise toward whatever faint light penetrates the surface during calm periods, converting it into the organic compounds that start the food chain. In deeper water they're replaced by Ventplant, but in the Twilight Shelves, Smallplant blooms are what drive the diel migration: they rise, Smallmeat follows, then Meatmeat, then everything else. The Osminoki track this movement as their primary clock (see Timekeeping). Newly hatched fry graze on Smallplant films coating nearby surfaces before they're large enough to chase anything.

#### Smallmeat

*"Tiny Food That Eats The Invisible Food And Feeds The Food We Eat"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *0.5–3mm* | **Analog:** *Zooplankton* |

Drifting grazers too small to see individually, feeding on Smallplant and converting it into the animal protein that sustains everything above them. Where Smallmeat congregates, Meatmeat follows; where Meatmeat swarms, everything else hunts. Adults don't eat them directly, but fry filter Smallmeat from the water as their first real prey, practicing the jet-and-grab reflexes they'll need for faster targets.

#### Meatmeat

*"The Many Little Ones Who Feed That Which We Hunt"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *1–5mm* | **Analog:** *Krill* |

The base of the food chain and absolutely everywhere. Meatmeat graze on Smallmeat in bioluminescent clouds so dense they create visible light-shows drifting through the water column, a "living snow" that rises and falls with the diel migration. Growing fry graduate to snatching Meatmeat once they're fast enough, and the transition from filter-grazing to active hunting is one of the first milestones of early development. Adults leave them alone, but seasonal shifts in Meatmeat density change the flavor profile of every predator above them. The meat that makes the meat.

#### Shimmerfish

*"Shimmering Food In Great Numbers For Which We Give Thanks"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *15–30cm* | **Analog:** *Tunicates* |

Transparent free-swimming organisms hosting symbiotic bioluminescent bacteria, swimming in schools of thousands. Each individual has a distributed nervous system that links into the school, letting the whole group act as one flickering organism. Mild and slightly sweet; the staple protein most Osminoki grow up eating.

#### Fastmeat

*"Comforting Food That Swims Faster Than You And In Greater Numbers"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *30–80cm* | **Analog:** *Tuna* |

Sleek, torpedo-shaped, muscular swimmers covered in mirror-reflective scales that confuse predators by scattering bioluminescent light in all directions. They swim in tight geometric formations that shift and fold like living origami. Firm, mild, reliable protein and the everyday catch. Their flesh changes color post-mortem, fading from silver to dull grey, which Osminoki use as a freshness indicator.

#### Crunchclaw

*"Quick Safe Food With Many Legs And Delicious Snapping Claws"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Common* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *5–30cm* | **Analog:** *Crabs* |

Six legs, two claws, and an attitude. Crunchclaw communicate by drumming their claws against rocks in complex rhythmic patterns, and they're notorious for stealing unattended tools. Many an Osminoki has returned to find their shell-knife dragged into a crevice by an opportunistic Crunchclaw. Sweet, rich, satisfying, and their shells contain trace metals useful for simple crafting.

#### Flatmeat

*"Reliable Food That Hides At The Bottom And Waits For Us To Take Them"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Common* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *20–50cm* | **Analog:** *Flounder* |

Extremely flat bottom-dwellers that bury themselves in sediment and wait with the patience of stone. Osminoki find them by chemoreception, tasting the sediment with their suckers until they detect the telltale chemical signature of a hidden Flatmeat. Bland but filling, considered comfort food, and some individuals are centuries old and enormous.

#### Siftshell

*"Safe Food That Sifts The Sediment To Form Beautiful Orbs"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Availability:** *Common* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *5–20cm* | **Analog:** *Clams* |

Burrowing filter-feeders that process sediment into mineral pearls. Chewy, salty, and reliably found in the same beds generation after generation. Occasionally a Siftshell incorporates Memory-Stone seed-crystals into its shell by accident, producing "echo-pearls" with faint EM signatures. These are curiosities, trade goods, and minor Abbey research subjects.

#### Rockgripper

*"Safe Food That Grips The Rocks Whose Half-Shells Can Remove Them"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *2–10cm* | **Analog:** *Limpets* |

Their suction rivals Osminoki suckers, and removing them from rocks is a patience exercise that doubles as a game for juveniles. Edible but unremarkable; the real value is in the shells, which make excellent scraping tools (helpful when harvesting Rockgrippers themselves), containers, cutting edges, and trade markers.

#### Slowforest

*"Slow Food That Grows Its Own Forest From Light And Heat"*

| **Type:** *Colonial* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *Variable* | **Analog:** *Pyrosomes + sargassum* |

Mobile, semi-motile colonial algae-animals that blur the line between plant and animal. Photosynthetic where faint light reaches them, chemosynthetic in deeper water, they creep along at geological pace and are harvested like crops. Earthy and mineral-rich, they serve as the primary carbohydrate source for most Osminoki communities.

#### Crunchmeat

*"Slow Safe Food Whose Claws Must Be Bound While Trapped"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Uncommon* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *20–50cm* | **Analog:** *Lobsters* |

Slow but tough, a prize catch worth every patient effort. Their claws can sever tentacle tips if not immediately bound with kelp fiber after capture, which gives them a reputation as "worthy" food. You earn a Crunchmeat. Firm, sweet, delicious, and the centerpiece of any communal feast.

#### Kelpgrazer

*"Slow Gentle Grazers Of The Kelp Whose Milk We Take And Whose Meat We Earn"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Uncommon* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *2–4m* | **Analog:** *Manatee + walrus* |

Massive, docile herbivores that drift through Kelp Forests cropping fronds with flat grinding plates. They grow painfully slowly, which means hunting them is a rare occasion reserved for large communal feasts; most of the time, Osminoki milk them instead, collecting a thick, nutrient-dense secretion that supplements diets during lean periods. Kelpgrazers tolerate the milking process with the patient indifference of something that knows nothing here can eat it quickly.

#### Crunchsnack

*"Plentiful Food Found Glowing Near And Crisped Upon Deep Hot Vents"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Availability:** *Common* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *2–5cm* | **Analog:** *Vent shrimp + scaly-foot snail* |

Small vent-dwellers with black carapaces that glow faintly red when warm from vent proximity. Roasted near hydrothermal vents for a crispy, nutty, metallic snack (eat too many and you'll regret it). Their shells absorb vent minerals that cause them to glow, and eating a handful causes brief bioluminescent flickers in the consumer's chromatophores, which juveniles find hilarious.

#### Crunchtreat

*"Crystal Food Rare To See Adrift So Make Sure You Catch And Share It"*

| **Type:** *Crystal* | **Role:** *Decomposer* | **Availability:** *Rare* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones (free-floating)* | **Size:** *5–15cm* | **Analog:** *Radiolarians + larvaceans* |

Free-floating organisms with hard crystalline exoskeletons that feed by filtering and crystallizing dissolved minerals. Semi-transparent geometric structures that sparkle in bioluminescent light. Basically living geodes. Crunchy, salty, and a rare luxury worth sharing. They also serve as free-floating decomposers, processing dead organic matter in the water column (see [The Crystalline Decomposers](#the-crystalline-decomposers)).

#### Grubmeat

*"Unappetizing Food In Sediment With Many Segments And Taste Of Sulfur"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Decomposer* | **Availability:** *Abundant* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Midnight Plains, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *10–50cm* | **Analog:** *Polychaete worms* |

Segmented detritivores that live in sediment and taste exactly as sulfurous and bitter as they look. Incredibly nutritious despite the flavor, and they can enter cryptobiosis for decades, meaning there are always Grubmeat somewhere if you're desperate enough to dig. Starvation food: what you eat when nothing else remains, and what you're grateful for when that moment comes.

## What Shares Our Waters

The living world that sustains, shelters, cleans, and sometimes confuses.

### Flora & Habitat

#### Kelp Forest

*"Waving Forest Of Safety And Light Where We Shelter And Slumber"*

| **Type:** *Flora* | **Role:** *Shelter* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *Fronds 10–40m tall* | **Analog:** *Kelp (duh)* |

Dense forests of thick, rubbery fronds whose bioluminescent tips guide navigation through the perpetual dark. Primary habitat for most Osminoki: shelter, hunting ground, nursery, and bedroom. Hundreds of smaller species make their homes in the canopy, and the fronds themselves are woven into rope, nets, and clothing. When Osminoki say they're going home, they usually mean a Kelp Forest.

#### Pressurebloom Forest

*"Tall Living Branches That Bloom Below And Shelter Us"*

| **Type:** *Colonial* | **Role:** *Shelter* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Midnight Plains, Crushing Deep (pressure boundaries)* | **Size:** *50m+ tall* | **Analog:** *Siphonophore colony + giant kelp* |

Vertical "trees" formed by colonial organisms at pressure boundaries, where each "branch" is a different symbiotic species and the entire forest is one interconnected superorganism. Their roots tap into warmer deep currents, and seasonal "blooming" creates temporary light shows visible for kilometers, cascading waves of bioluminescence that mark the closest thing Osminok has to seasons. Used as landmarks, calendar markers, and reliable meeting points.

#### Ventplant

*"Living Mats Upon Hot Vents That Eat Poison And Make It Into Food"*

| **Type:** *Flora* | **Role:** *Producer* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Vent Gardens, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *Mats spanning meters* | **Analog:** *Chemosynthetic bacteria* |

Thick bacterial mats coating the rocks around hydrothermal vents, harvesting chemical energy from hydrogen sulfide, methane, and dissolved metals. Where Smallplant uses light, Ventplant uses poison, and together they sustain virtually all complex life on the planet. The mats smell terrible and taste worse, but every Vent Gardens ecosystem begins here. Ventplant is also what makes Crunchsnack taste the way it does, for better and worse.

### Sessile Life, Scavengers, & Cleaners

#### Clingtube

*"Those Who Mark The Way With The Clinging Corpses Of Their Kin"*

| **Type:** *Colonial* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *Individual tubes 1–10m, colonies span hectares* | **Analog:** *Barnacles* |

Colonial filter-feeders building tall calcified tubes harder than bone. Dead spires persist for centuries, forming navigation highways that entire communities orient around. Living ones glow faintly with absorbed minerals, and their formations serve as the Osminoki equivalent of road signs. The tubes are also harvested as structural building material: reliable, abundant, and strong.

#### Frilltube

*"Tall Frilled Tubes That Bloom From The Vent-Rocks And Sway In The Heat"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *30cm–3m* | **Analog:** *Giant tube worms + feather-duster worms* |

Sessile worms anchored to vent-rock in tough mineral tubes, extending elaborate gill-plumes into the current to filter-feed. Dense colonies turn vent fields into surreal gardens, the plumes retracting instantly when disturbed and slowly blooming again once the danger passes. They house symbiotic chemobacteria inside their bodies, letting them feed even when nothing drifts past. Osminoki juveniles sometimes compete to see who can make an entire colony retract the fastest.

#### Ventshell

*"Many Shelled Ones Near The Vents That Are Not Worth Eating"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *1–15cm (varies widely)* | **Analog:** *Vent mussels + scaly-foot gastropods* |

A catch-all name for the dozens of shelled species clustering around hydrothermal vents, filter-feeding on Ventplant and dissolved minerals. Most are too small, too tough, too toxic, or too foul-tasting to bother with; the Osminoki's taxonomic indifference is right there in the name. If it has a shell, lives near a vent, and isn't Crunchsnack, it's a Ventshell. Some species incorporate iron sulfide into their shells, making them functionally metallic, and these are occasionally harvested for the shell material rather than the meat inside.

#### Trench-Crawler

*"Those Who Crawl The Trenches In The Dark And Deep"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Scavenger* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Midnight Plains, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *30cm–2m* | **Analog:** *Giant isopods, sea spiders* |

Scavengers of every shape and too many legs. Giant isopod-like things, multi-limbed crawlers, dog-sized spidery nightmares. Too numerous to avoid and too unappetizing to bother hunting. They follow Osminoki hoping for scraps from hunts, and can be temporarily warded off with a blast of ink. Some vent communes have semi-tamed populations that serve as garbage disposal, which is either practical or disgusting depending on who you ask.

#### Siftslug

*"Those Who Sluggishly Sift The Sediment Only To Form More Sediment On And On Into Forever"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Decomposer* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Midnight Plains, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *20–100cm* | **Analog:** *Sea cucumbers* |

Slug-like detritivores that can eviscerate and regrow their own organs when threatened, which is both viscerally upsetting and deeply unnecessary. They produce massive quantities of processed sediment that becomes the "soil" other organisms root in, and their trails create navigable paths through murky water. Taste horrible. Essential part of the ecosystem.

#### Crunchcleaner

*"Quick Ones With Careful Claws Who Pick Our Gills And Skin Clean Of Pests"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Symbiont* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *2–8cm* | **Analog:** *Cleaner shrimp* |

Tiny, brightly bioluminescent creatures that establish cleaning stations on prominent rocks and coral formations, advertising their services with rhythmic light pulses. Larger organisms visit these stations to have parasites, dead skin, and gill debris picked away by precise, delicate claws. The relationship is genuinely mutualistic: Crunchcleaners get fed, and their clients get relief from the constant threat of Gillworm larvae and Pressure Mite infestation. Some Osminoki communities maintain dedicated cleaning stations the way others maintain tool caches.

#### Clingfish

*"Small Cleaning Fish That Cling To Larger Beasts But Will Not Trust Our Tentacles"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Symbiont* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *5–15cm* | **Analog:** *Bothriolepis + lamprey* |

Armored, jawless, and built like a leftover from an earlier age of life. Clingfish have bony head-shields, articulated plate-covered forelimbs that lock onto the rigid surfaces of larger creatures, and a rasping disc-mouth that scrapes away parasites and dead tissue. They'll clamp onto Kelpgrazers, Shallowskates, even Sleekfish, but they can't get purchase on Osminoki skin: too soft, too flexible, nothing rigid to grip. The Osminoki insist the rejection is personal. Inedible (all armor, no meat) but valued as cleaners for tamed animals, and their prehistoric stubbornness is considered endearing.

### The Stinging Gardens

#### Stingplant

*"Stinging Growths That Welcome Only Us Among Their Killing Arms"*

| **Type:** *Flora* | **Role:** *Predator (Sessile)* | **Threat:** *None (to Osminoki)* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *20cm–2m across* | **Analog:** *Sea anemone* |

Sessile predators ringed with tentacles loaded with nematocysts powerful enough to paralyze anything that brushes against them. Everything on Osminok gives Stingplants a wide berth except the Osminoki, who are completely immune: quick calories and ready-made shelter from predators meant that individuals less bothered by the sting bred more successfully, and thousands of generations later, the tolerance is species-wide. An Osminoki can reach straight through a Stingplant's lethal waving tentacles, scoop out the Stingfish eggs nestled at the base, and eat them on the spot. This is considered one of the small, satisfying perks of living on a planet that is otherwise trying to kill you at all times.

#### Stingfish

*"Poisonous Ones Who Hide Among The Stinging Arms Where Only We Can Reach"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *3–10cm* | **Analog:** *Clownfish + scorpion* |

Brilliantly colored and armed with a segmented tail ending in a venom bulb, plus beaded toxin sacs along their fins that make them look like living jewelry and hurt like nothing else. Stingfish live exclusively among Stingplant tentacles, protected by a mucus coating that neutralizes the nematocysts. Their eggs are a rich, creamy delicacy that Osminoki prize specifically because only they can harvest them. Reach in, ignore the toxin, pluck the egg clusters, and munch away! The fish themselves are not eaten (too pointy, too venomous, and frankly not worth the trouble when the eggs are right there), but left undisturbed they produce new clutches year-round, making every Stingplant a self-replenishing snack supply. Stingfish seem to find the entire arrangement quite unfair.

### Free-Swimming Fauna

#### Nursemouth

*"Mothers Who Impress And Humble Us With Their Nursery Mouths"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Symbiont* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *30–60cm* | **Analog:** *Mouthbrooding cichlids* |

Mouthbrooders that carry young in throat pouches and feed them with nutrient slime, a form of proto-lactation that fascinated the first xenobiologists to study Osminok's ecology. Fiercely protective parents who will attack creatures many times their size. Culturally taboo to hunt; the Osminoki see parallels to their own broodguard system in Nursemouths and respect parental care they cannot provide themselves.

#### Firmwater

*"Those Made Of Firm Water Who Drift In Numbers To Warn Us"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *10cm–2m* | **Analog:** *Jellyfish* |

Bell-and-tentacle drifters with mild stings that are painful but rarely dangerous to adult Osminoki. Their real significance is ecological: mass blooms predict incoming megastorm intensification, making them a living early-warning system for weather changes. Some species are symbiotic with Thoughtwater, amplifying their EM fields into strange composite signals that Osminoki find uncomfortable or nauseating.

#### Longlightning

*"Those With Small Lightning Who Skulk In Deep Stone Cracks"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *1–3m* | **Analog:** *Eels* |

Crevice-dwelling predators with mild electric shocks, considered pests because they raid Slowforest patches. Their electric discharges are used in juvenile Osminoki games of reflexes and daring: dart your tentacle past the crevice without getting zapped. They nest in bonded pairs, one guarding the den while the other hunts, and they're annoyingly clever about avoiding traps.

#### Sleekfish

*"Swift Hunters Who Think Together In Packs And Earn Our Respect In Open Water"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Pack)* | **Threat:** *Moderate* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *2–4m* | **Analog:** *Dolphin + mako shark* |

Fast, intelligent pack hunters coordinating through bioluminescent flank-signals and low-frequency clicks, operating in pods of twenty or more. They hunt Fastmeat and Shimmerfish primarily, driving prey into tight balls before striking in rapid alternation. Osminoki and Sleekfish generally give each other professional courtesy; both are semi-social, intelligent hunters, and neither benefits from a fight. Occasionally a pod shadows an Osminoki hunting party and picks off whatever scatters, which is tolerated with the understanding that the reverse happens too.

#### Fightfish

*"Fierce Swift Ones With Blades For Faces Who Keep Their Distance As We Keep Ours"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Moderate* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *1.5–3m* | **Analog:** *Swordfish + barracuda* |

Solitary ambush predators built for explosive acceleration, with elongated rostrums reinforced by crystallized mineral deposits hard enough to punch through shell and bone. They stake out territories along Kelp Forest edges and pressure boundaries, striking at prey that wanders too close; miss the first charge, and they rarely pursue. Osminoki respect the territorial boundaries and don't linger where Fightfish lurk, and the arrangement works for everyone.

#### Bloatfish

*"Those Who Bloat Rapidly When Startled And Taste Poorly"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *10–30cm* | **Analog:** *Pufferfish* |

Inflate AND flash bright warning patterns simultaneously when threatened, which is startling every single time no matter how many you've seen. Bony plates and mildly toxic skin make them taste terrible even to desperate predators. Rarely eaten, but sometimes grabbed and thrown as improvised distractions during hunts, since the flash-and-bloat display draws attention away from the hunter.

#### Glitterscale

*"Glittering Scales Waiting Alone Peacefully And Stare At The Stars"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Ice Shelves* | **Size:** *2–5m* | **Analog:** *Oarfish* |

Thin, flat, solitary, and draped in bioluminescent warning stripes that ripple along their bodies in slow hypnotic patterns. Barely any meat and pointless to catch. But Glitterscales are the subject of Osminoki poetry and contemplation. During rare storm breaks when light penetrates the upper water, they drift motionless near the surface, scales shimmering, and genuinely appear to be watching the sky. Whether they're thinking anything is unknowable. The Osminoki find them beautiful anyway.

#### Shallowskate

*"Those Whose Blood Runs Warm In The Shallows Through Which They Glide"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Prey* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves (upper reaches)* | **Size:** *50cm–1m* | **Analog:** *Leatherback turtle + manta ray* |

Endothermic, meaning warm-blooded in a world where almost nothing is, which makes them a genuine biological oddity here (viviparity and live young are common enough in Osminok's death-ocean). Smooth rubbery skin, warm to the touch, and preferring the shallowest accessible waters. Juvenile Osminoki sometimes ride them for fun, which the Shallowskates tolerate with apparent indifference. A cultural curiosity rather than a food source.

### Companion Species

#### Forestfish

*"Quiet Ones Who Wrap Their Tails In Kelp And Sometimes Choose To Stay With Us"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves (Kelp Forests)* | **Size:** *10–40cm* | **Analog:** *Seahorse + sea krait* |

Slender, prehensile-tailed creatures that anchor themselves to Kelp Forest fronds and sway with the current, nearly invisible when still. Ambush predators of smaller organisms, striking with surprising speed before returning to their perch. Some develop a tolerance for Osminoki presence and can be coaxed into a semi-tamed state with consistent feeding, serving as pest control in living spaces. The Osminoki are careful to note that Forestfish choose to stay; they are never truly domesticated.

#### Fastfriend

*"Fast Loyal Ones Who Learn Our Calls And Hunt Beside Us For Shared Catch"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Pack)* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Ice Shelves* | **Size:** *0.7–3m; polar variants up to 5m* | **Analog:** *Seal + Clidastes (polar: leopard seal + mosasaur)* |

Sleek, powerful, (blessedly) warm-blooded pursuit predators with six paddle-flippers and a mouth full of conical teeth built for gripping rather than shearing. In the wild they hunt in small family groups; in Osminoki communities they're the closest thing to a domesticated hunting animal, and they are treated accordingly.

Bonded Fastfriends receive names, are adorned with shell-bead jewelry threaded through their dorsal ridges, and get the choicest scraps from every catch before anyone else eats. They learn specific EM-pulse signals for "go," "return," "herd left," and "bring it here," and experienced pairs develop a shorthand so personal that other Osminoki can't replicate the commands. The relationship is genuine partnership: Fastfriends sleep curled against their bonded partner, refuse to hunt with strangers, and will search for a missing Osminoki long after practicality suggests they should stop. Losing one is mourned openly, and the shell-beads are kept.

Three regional variants exist. Pygmy Fastfriends (~0.7–1m) are too small for serious hunting but serve as companions for juveniles and pest control in tight living spaces where a full-sized partner can't maneuver. The temperate standard (1.5–3m) is the working hunter most Osminoki bond with. Polar variants found under the Ice Shelves run up to five meters long and significantly heavier, built for wrestling oversized prey against the ice ceiling; polar Osminoki ride them, gripping the dorsal ridges with all eight tentacles while their Fastfriend corkscrews through freezing water after something that also doesn't want to be caught.

#### Glowspiral

*"Gentle Glowing Spirals Who Light Our Homes And Ask Only Algae In Return"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Symbiont* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *5–30cm* | **Analog:** *Ammonite (cultivated)* |

Docile, slow-moving shelled organisms with a broad flat foot and a logarithmic spiral shell lined with bioluminescent tissue that glows a steady warm blue-green. Osminoki have cultivated Glowspirals for thousands of cycles, selectively encouraging the brightest lineages until they've become living lanterns: reliable, portable, and content to graze on algae in whatever space they're placed. Clusters mounted on walls, tucked into crevices, or carried on journeys provide the soft, constant light that makes a space feel inhabited. A home without Glowspirals is not yet a home.

**Crunchcleaner** (see [Sessile Life, Scavengers, & Cleaners](#sessile-life-scavengers-cleaners)) also qualifies as a companion species; Osminoki communities maintain dedicated cleaning stations and the relationship is fully mutualistic.

### Oddities & Environmental Hazards

#### Echobloom

*"Crystal Blooms Whose High Echoing Song Lets Them See"*

| **Type:** *Flora* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *20–60cm per bloom, fields span kilometers* | **Analog:** *Waterwheel plant + glass sponge* |

Sessile crystal flowers whose "petals" are resonant sonar chambers that echolocate to detect small prey and snap shut to capture it. Not dangerous to Osminoki (far too large to trap), but vast Echobloom fields create "singing meadows" audible as a high, ringing tone that carries for great distances. Harvested sonar-chambers are used as acoustic tools and musical instruments, and navigating by the sound of an Echobloom field is a basic Osminoki skill.

#### Facefish

*"Those With Faces Like Ours Who Watch Us Back And Must Never Be Hunted"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *15–60cm* | **Analog:** *Cuttlefish* |

Ten flexible limbs, large forward-facing eyes, chromatophore-rich skin capable of complex color signaling, and a body plan that occasionally produces arrangements unsettlingly reminiscent of an Osminoki face. Hunting them is one of the oldest and most universal taboos; no commune teaches it, no circumstance justifies it. Whether the resemblance reflects convergent evolution or a distant common ancestor is a question the Abbey has investigated for thousands of cycles without resolution. Facefish, for their part, seem equally fascinated by Osminoki, often hovering at the edge of bioluminescent light and watching with an intensity that does nothing to settle the question.

#### Thoughtwater

*"Those Made Of Firm Water Whose Lights May Calm Or Anger Us"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Symbiont* | **Threat:** *Low–Moderate* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *5–50cm* | **Analog:** *None* |

Generate powerful EM fields that directly affect Osminoki emotional states. Red species broadcast aggressive, warning-like signals and are avoided. Blue species broadcast calm, safe-feeling signals and are kept in living spaces as mood regulators. Purple species create confusion and disorientation, and whether they're doing this deliberately is a subject of intense scientific and religious debate at the Abbey. The idea that a brainless drifter can reach into your mind and change how you feel is... unsettling.

#### Slimespiral

*"Slimy Spirals Of The Vents Whose Goop Brings Calm And Colors And Must Be Shared With Care"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Filter-feeder* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Vent Gardens* | **Size:** *20–50cm* | **Analog:** *Nudibranch + hagfish (convergent slime)* |

Slow-grazing mollusks with tall conical shells, closely related to Glowspirals but diverged sharply in survival strategy: where Glowspirals glow, Slimespirals ooze. When disturbed, they produce copious psychoactive mucus that scrambles predator nervous systems, and the conical shell is too pointed and slick for jaws to grip, liable to lodge in a throat or pierce a gill on the way down. Most predators learn to leave them alone after one attempt. Osminoki, whose suckered tentacles and opposable thumbs grip through slime and handle awkward shell geometry with ease, are the only species that regularly bothers them on purpose; controlled exposure to the mucus produces euphoria, heightened social bonding, and vivid chromatophore color-shifting that Vent Garden communities enjoy in carefully planned group sessions.

#### Blacksludge

*"Black Sludge On The Rocks That You Should Not Touch"*

| **Type:** *Mineral* | **Role:** *Hazard* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Crushing Deep, Forgotten Depths* | **Size:** *Patches of cm to m* | **Analog:** *Radiotrophic fungi + endoliths* |

No detectable metabolism. They harvest energy from ambient radiation via quantum effects, grow approximately one cell per year, and some specimens are millions of years old. They look like black tar on rocks, are potentially the oldest discrete organisms on the planet, and are barely alive by any definition; theoretically immortal. Abbey scholars have debated for thousands of cycles whether Blacksludge qualifies as alive, as dead, or as something that pre-dates the distinction.

#### Geyserstone

*"Gifts Of Stone That Float From The Geysers"*

| **Type:** *Geological* | **Role:** *Hazard* | **Threat:** *Moderate* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Vent Gardens, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *Variable (eruptions span 10–100m)* | **Analog:** *None* |

Not organisms, but pockets of supercritical CO2 trapped in deep sediments. Osminoki tap controlled seeps for chemical energy, buoyancy aids, and territory markers. Wild eruptions are another matter entirely: scalding chemical burns from superheated fluid, and the eruption plume itself can tumble and disorient anything nearby. An uncontrolled Geyserstone eruption is an evacuation signal. Get clear and don't look back.

### The Crystalline Decomposers

Bacteria, archaea, and scavengers like Siftslug and Grubmeat handle most of Osminok's decomposition. But the planet's most distinctive recyclers are the Crystalline Decomposers, mineral-organic hybrid organisms that crystallize organic matter instead of breaking it down enzymatically. They incorporate organic molecules into growing mineral matrices, grow crystal networks through dead tissue, use dissolved metals as structural material, and release simpler nutrients as byproduct.

#### Voidcoral

*"Dark Strong Coral That Gleams In Our Light"*

| **Type:** *Crystal* | **Role:** *Decomposer* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Crushing Deep, Bone Yards* | **Size:** *Reef structures spanning kilometers* | **Analog:** *Black coral + ferromanganese nodules* |

The primary decomposer network on Osminok, building structures from precipitated metals (iron, manganese) instead of calcium. Metallic black, red, and purple formations that grow at centimeters per century, slowly incorporating carcasses into reef structure over decades. Chemosynthetic reef-builders thriving in total darkness, and also the Osminoki's primary source of metal, carefully mined from ancient formations where the mineral content is richest.

**Crunchtreat** (see [What We Eat](#what-we-eat)) also serves as a free-floating recycler, processing dead organic matter in the water column and crystallizing dissolved compounds before they reach the seafloor.

#### Bonebloom

*"Crystal Blooms That Feast Upon Bones And Let Us Use Their Insides"*

| **Type:** *Crystal* | **Role:** *Decomposer* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Bone Yards* | **Size:** *10–60cm per bloom* | **Analog:** *Osedax worms + anthodites* |

Grows on carcasses in Bone Yards, producing translucent crystal "flowers" whose color reveals what they're feeding on: blue from copper-based hemocyanin blood, red from iron-rich muscle, white from calcium shell and bone, black from sulfur-heavy organs. A mature Bonebloom colony on a large carcass is genuinely beautiful, a garden of colored glass growing from death. Harvested for mineral supplements, tools, and trade goods.

#### Crustsludge

*"Green And Blue Sludge On The Rocks That Is Safe To Touch"*

| **Type:** *Crystal* | **Role:** *Decomposer* | **Threat:** *None* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *Surface crusts, mm to cm thick* | **Analog:** *Microbial mats + ferromanganese crust* |

Multispecies partnerships between bacteria and mineral organisms forming living geological crusts. They coat rocks in thin, colorful films that slowly process organic residue from the water, and they're one of the few organisms on Osminok that are genuinely boring. Safe to touch, as the name notes (which is worth saying on a planet where touching the wrong rock-coating can blind your EM senses).

#### Memory-Stone

*"Gifts Of Stone That Record Memories From Within"*

| **Type:** *Crystal* | **Role:** *Symbiont* | **Threat:** *Low* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *5mm to 15cm (individual crystals)* | **Analog:** *Otoliths + myxozoan cysts* |

Crystalline organisms that grow inside living tissue, symbiotic in some species and pathogenic in others. They incorporate their host's biochemical information into their crystal structure as they grow, and when the host dies, the Memory-Stone preserves a chemical record of that life. The Abbey studies ancient Memory-Stones to reconstruct extinct species, trace evolutionary lineages, and occasionally argue about whether the crystals constitute a form of memory or merely data.

## What Hurts Us

Organized from nuisance to nightmare.

### Parasites & Environmental Hazards

#### Gillworm

*"Those Who Hide Silently In Your Gills And Must Be Brine-Purged"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Parasite* | **Threat:** *Moderate* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *All zones* | **Size:** *Larvae microscopic, adults 1–2cm* | **Analog:** *Gill flukes* |

Free-swimming larvae that detect electrical activity from kilometers away and home in on Osminoki gill currents. They burrow into gill tissue, anchor with hook-like appendages, and mature into blood-feeding worms over several malton units. Infected Osminoki notice the taste of their gill-water changing to a metallic, copper-tinged flavor. That's the first reliable symptom. Treatment is a brine-purge: flushing the gills with high-salinity water that the worms can't tolerate, painful but effective. Left untreated, a heavy infestation slowly suffocates.

#### Pressure Mite

*"Many Little Ones In The Deep Who Burrow Into Skin And Burst"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Parasite* | **Threat:** *High* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Crushing Deep, Living Trenches* | **Size:** *Microscopic* | **Analog:** *Demodex mites (pressure-obligate)* |

Organisms adapted to extreme high-pressure zones below 7,000m, dying instantly if pressure drops below 600 bars. They burrow under Osminoki skin at depth, feeding on dermal fluids, and the infection is painless enough to go unnoticed. The danger arrives on ascent: as the host rises and pressure drops, the mites expand and rupture, creating lesions that burst open like tiny pressure-wounds across the skin. This effectively traps the infected Osminoki at depth, because ascending means agony and staying means a worsening infestation. Treatment requires careful extraction at depth by a skilled healer.

*Never ascend quickly with unexplained skin irritation.*

#### Stingmat

*"Those Who Disrupt Our Senses But Cannot Eat Us Because They Are Too Slow And Flat"*

| **Type:** *Colonial* | **Role:** *Parasite* | **Threat:** *Moderate* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *Mats span 1–10m* | **Analog:** *Electric ray + cyanobacterial mat* |

Looks exactly like a harmless bacterial mat until you touch it. Stingmat is a colonial organism that produces coordinated electrical pulses, and when disturbed, it releases clouds of stinging spores that jam Osminoki EM field sensing, causing temporary sensory "blindness" that can last marbecs. The spores disperse on the current, meaning the disruption zone expands well beyond the mat itself. Not directly lethal, but losing your EM senses in open water with predators nearby is a death sentence by proxy.

*If the rocks feel wrong, back away slowly.*

#### Razorbloom

*"Many Little Ones Who Are Beautiful But Cut Us When They Bloom"*

| **Type:** *Colonial* | **Role:** *Hazard* | **Threat:** *High* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *Blooms span cubic meters (individual polyps 1mm)* | **Analog:** *Pyrosomes + glass sponge spicules* |

Drifting colonial organisms whose bioluminescent displays are genuinely beautiful, cascading waves of color that pulse through the bloom like a living light-show. The beauty is the problem. Each polyp is covered in silicate spines finer than hair, and swimming through a bloom causes thousands of micro-cuts across every exposed surface. Blood in the water. Which attracts Bloodswarm. The bloom itself is harmless in the long run; the chain reaction it triggers is what kills.

*If you see something beautiful drifting toward you, swim the other way.*

#### Trickfish

*"Those Who Trick Our Eyes And Minds And Must Always Be Avoided"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Parasite* | **Threat:** *High* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves* | **Size:** *10–15cm* | **Analog:** *Cuttlefish + false cleaner wrasse* |

Small and harmless-looking, which is the point. Trickfish produce hypnotic bioluminescent patterns that exploit Osminoki visual and EM processing, creating a disorientation effect that's genuinely terrifying because the victim doesn't realize it's happening. You feel calm. Curious. You want to look closer. Meanwhile the Trickfish is feeding on your mucus membranes and skin secretions while you float there dazed, perfectly content. Victims describe snapping out of it with no memory of lost time and a raw, irritated feeling across their skin.

*If a companion is staring at nothing and won't respond, drag them away.*

### Cunning Hunters

#### Bloodswarm

*"Thousands Of Swarming Teeth Dancing In Blood"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Pack)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *~60cm individually, swarms in thousands* | **Analog:** *Piranha* |

Individually, a Bloodswarm fish is a coward: small, bony, skittish, and easily scared off. But blood in the water triggers exponential swarming behavior that transforms them into something else entirely. A single injured organism can attract hundreds within martrons and thousands within marbecs, each individual arriving hungrier and more aggressive than the last. The swarm strips flesh with mechanical efficiency, every fish taking a single bite and peeling away to make room for the next. They don't fight over food; there's always more food. Cauterizing wounds doesn't help once the swarm has formed; even trace chemical signatures in the water maintain the frenzy.

*Ink cloud and immediate flight. Never fight. Blood from your attacker attracts more.*

#### Trapclaw

*"Colorful Ones Who Trap Their Territory And Strike With Mind Then Claw"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *3–5m* | **Analog:** *Mantis shrimp* |

The intelligence debate around Trapclaws has consumed Abbey scholars for generations: they use rocks as tools, wedging them into crevices to block escape routes before attacking. They coordinate ambush tactics with other Trapclaws via bioluminescent secretions that mark territory boundaries, hunting lanes, and "kill zones." Their striking appendages can shatter shells and crack stone, delivering blows fast enough to cavitate water. They're vibrantly colored, territorial, and will chase fleeing prey for kilometers with what can only be described as spite. Some Osminoki insist they've seen Trapclaws watching and waiting, learning patrol routes, studying hunting patterns, setting traps specifically designed for Osminoki anatomy. Whether that constitutes sapience or just very good predatory instinct is a question nobody wants answered.

*Avoid their territories. If you must pass through, leave an offering of food at the boundary and move quickly.*

### The Great Hunters

#### Darkmaw

*"Untrustworthy Light Dancing Upon The Seabed Above A Dark Maw"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Lure)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *40–60m* | **Analog:** *Anglerfish* |

A Darkmaw buries itself in Midnight Plains sediment and waits for weeks, perfectly still, perfectly hidden, with only its bioluminescent lure-appendages visible above the surface. The lure doesn't just produce light; it mimics the EM signature of safe organisms, broadcasting "calm" and "food" signals that Osminoki EM senses interpret as safety. Knowing this doesn't help as much as it should; the mimicry is good enough that experienced hunters still second-guess themselves. When prey drifts close enough, expandable jaws open wide enough to swallow organisms up to a third of the Darkmaw's own body length in a single convulsive gulp. There is no escape once you're within range. The strike is faster than an Osminoki's jet-propulsion reflex.

*Avoid the open plains. Move in groups. If you see a beautiful light in a place where nothing should be glowing, that's why it's glowing.*

#### Razorback

*"Sharp Dorsal Hunters All Clever And Quick"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Pack)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *25–40m* | **Analog:** *Mosasaur* |

Massive predators with serrated dorsal ridges that slice through anything unlucky enough to be above them when they surge upward. Razorbacks hunt in packs of three to six, using echolocation to coordinate formation changes during pursuit, click-patterns that shift the pack's geometry in real time, herding prey into kill-boxes. They're fast, hitting 40+ km/h in short bursts, and smart enough to use terrain features to cut off escape routes. The pack communicates constantly, their echolocation clicks echoing through the water like a tightening net. The one mercy: they're loud, and an Osminoki who hears the clicks has a few martrons to reach complex terrain where the pack's coordination advantage breaks down.

*Ink cloud, dive for the nearest crevice or Kelp Forest, and make yourself as small as possible.*

#### Crushing Coil

*"The Long One Lying In Wait To Curl And Coil To Crush Its Prey"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *70–100m long, 2–3m diameter* | **Analog:** *Oarfish + anaconda* |

The terrifying thing about a Crushing Coil is the silence. No EM signature. No echolocation. No bioluminescence. It patrols trench edges as a vast, dark shape that registers on exactly none of an Osminoki's primary senses until it strikes. When it does, it wraps around entire groups — not individuals, groups — and squeezes with the patient, inexorable pressure of geology. The coils tighten slowly enough that victims have time to understand what's happening, which is worse than a quick death. A single Crushing Coil can kill dozens at once.

*Never travel in tight groups in open water near trench edges. Spread out. If you feel something brush you that you can't see or sense, jet in any direction immediately.*

#### Screamer

*"Screaming Bloated Titan Who Bursts And Stuns"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Stalker)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Midnight Plains, Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *50–80m* | **Analog:** *Sperm whale* |

Bloated, bulbous, and strange-looking. A massive filter-feeder that hunts with focused sonic pulses powerful enough to rupture internal organs, cause hemorrhaging, and stun everything in a wide radius. The effect on Osminoki distributed nervous systems is particularly devastating: the pulse doesn't just stun, it scrambles, sending contradictory signals racing through the neural net so that tentacles seize, chromatophores fire randomly, and jet propulsion fails. The Screamer then filters stunned organisms from the water at its leisure. You can't dodge sound, and you can't outswim it, because the pulse travels faster than anything alive.

*Warning vibrations precede the pulse by a few ribecs. If you feel a low, building hum in your mantle, flee for deep shelter behind solid rock. Do not be in open water when the scream comes.*

#### Stormskate

*"Those Who Skate The Waves To Leap Towards The Storm And Deliver It"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Apex)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves (surface, 0–200m)* | **Size:** *50–80m wingspan, 30–40m whip-tail with 8–12m barb* | **Analog:** *Manta ray (superficial)* |

Flattened diamond-bodied rays that surf Osminok's megastorms, dark grey-black on top to vanish against the clouds and pale grey-blue underneath to blend with the water, their wing-edges traced in bioluminescent light. Conductive fin tissue acts as a living lightning rod, channeling strikes into a capacitor gland along the spine that stores roughly 500,000 volts. They actively seek out lightning, leaping 50–100 meters out of 100-meter waves to get struck mid-air. They hunt by skimming the surface during storms, diving to the upper 100m, impaling prey on their tail-barb, and discharging stored electricity through the wound, cooking prey from the inside out. Breeding aggregations of 200–500 individuals create a distinctive 20–50 Hz thrumming audible from depth, which is both a warning and, reportedly, an extraordinary sound. They're limited to the 0–200m zone where wing-based locomotion works, but within that range, nothing else matters.

*Stay deep during storms. If you hear the thrumming, go deeper.*

#### Stormsnake

*"Those Who Receive The Storm And Eat The Messengers"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Apex)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Twilight Shelves, Midnight Plains* | **Size:** *60–120m long, 4–6m diameter* | **Analog:** *Electric eel + sea snake* |

The predator that hunts the lightning-surfers. Serpentine and laterally compressed, a Stormsnake lives at 400–1,200m depth and senses electrical activity from 50+ km away, its electroreception specifically tuned to Stormskate discharge signatures. When it detects a hunting Stormskate above, it rises to position itself below the ray, activates a bioluminescent lure mimicking a Shimmerfish school to keep the ray's attention focused downward, then delivers an overwhelming electrical pulse exceeding a million joules, enough to stun even a lightning-adapted Stormskate. It coils around the immobilized ray's wings, drags it down past 600m where pressure trauma finishes the job, and swallows it whole. The arms race between these two species is remarkable: the ray's primary weapon (electricity) is useless against a creature that absorbs it.

*This one hunts Stormskates, not Osminoki. But if you're between a Stormsnake and its target when it surges upward at 60–80 km/h, that distinction won't save you.*

## What Waits Below

The deep abyss, 3,000m and beyond. What lives here does not belong in the world above.

#### Brine Ghost

*"Untrustworthy Light Dancing Above Brine Pools That Collects Us"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Lure)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Crushing Deep* | **Size:** *8–15m* | **Analog:** *Wobbegong + shrike* |

Flattened, ray-like, translucent grey-white, and found in deep brine pools between 3,000–8,000m. A Brine Ghost can actively regulate its internal salinity to match either normal seawater or hypersaline brine, hiding motionless in crystal-clear pools for malton units at a time. It cultivates prey by secreting salt plumes that attract mineral-feeding organisms, then lures predators to investigate with bioluminescent mimicry from below the invisible halocline boundary. Prey crosses into the brine, suffers instant osmotic shock, and the Brine Ghost rockets upward to wrap around the dying creature and accelerate the kill by secreting concentrated salt directly onto gills and skin. It stores perfectly preserved corpses at the bottom of the brine pool as a pantry. When threatened outside the pool, it can weaponize its salt-secretion as a cloud of hypersaline water. One per pool, highly territorial. Total population under 500.

#### Crawling Dark

*"Dark Tendrils Of Crystal That Crawl Forever To Trap The Foolish"*

| **Type:** *Crystal* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Extreme* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Forgotten Depths* | **Size:** *Kilometers-long networks, 50–100m tendrils* | **Analog:** *Armillaria fungus (convergent growth pattern but crystalline)* |

Not quite alive, not quite mineral. Kilometers-long crystalline "root" networks growing through the ocean floor at 10,000–15,000m, with tendrils extending upward into the water column. It feeds mostly on minerals and dissolved organics, but occasionally wraps passing organisms in irreversible crystal growth, absorbing their organic matter and leaving a mineral husk that becomes part of its structure. The entire deep ocean floor might be one interconnected organism. It moves imperceptibly slowly. But once you're touched, the crystal growth cannot be stopped.

#### Waterfall

*"Those Made Of Firm Water Who Pull You Down And Absorb You"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Ambush)* | **Threat:** *Existential* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Forgotten Depths* | **Size:** *200–400m (incredibly diffuse)* | **Analog:** *Giant phantom jelly (Stygiomedusa)* |

Organisms that require extreme pressure to survive, dying above 8,000m. Massive transparent clouds with pulsing organs visible inside, drifting through the 12,000–18,000m zone, nearly invisible against the darkness. They generate powerful suction currents that pull prey down to crushing depths where it dies, then slowly absorb dissolved tissues over malton units. Nearly impossible to detect, they create the sensation of "falling" even when swimming level, a current that gets stronger the more you fight it. The Osminoki have no other use for the word "waterfall."

#### Still-Singer

*"Those Whose Song Has Been Forgotten By Time But Not By Us"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Hazard* | **Threat:** *Existential* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Forgotten Depths* | **Size:** *500–1,000m+* | **Analog:** *None* |

Starsinger-ancestors that never fully developed cavitation organs, trapped at 12,000–18,000m where pressure adapted them beyond any hope of return to shallower water. Potentially millions of cycles old. They mostly filter-feed, drifting through the Forgotten Depths in slow, vast circuits, but when something large passes nearby, they instinctively try to jump. Their malformed organs can't open a cavitation bubble. Instead they create localized spacetime distortions that generate gravity wells, crushing and imploding anything nearby. They're in constant pain from endlessly trying and failing to jump. They *might* be sentient? The Abbey doesn't know. What the Abbey does know is that the Still-Singers remember something — some echo of the impulse that drove their descendants to break free of the ocean — and they spend their endless lives reaching for it and failing.

### The Light Eaters

*"The Unfathomable Darkness That Devours All Light And Hope"*

| **Type:** *Fauna* | **Role:** *Predator (Apex)* | **Threat:** *Existential* |
|---|---|---|
| **Biome:** *Forgotten Depths, Unknowable Deep* | **Size:** *1,000–?????m (still growing!)* | **Analog:** *None* |

There are always two. The Osminoki don't know this.

Elongated, segmented, and radially symmetrical with no top or bottom, each Light Eater is covered in thousands of semi-independent sensory tendrils or filaments spanning 5–20m apiece. Eight to twelve feeding orifices arranged in a spiral pattern ring the body, each 10–15m in diameter and lined with inward-pointing teeth. They move by peristaltic wave contractions at 10–50 km per malton unit through the 15,000m+ zone. Eyeless, they sense their hemisphere through echolocation at 5–20 Hz (infrasound, below Osminoki hearing), EM field detection spanning the entire hemisphere, pressure sensing reaching 1,000+ km, and chemical sensing via tendrils at parts per billion. Their coloration is deep black-purple, broken by anti-bioluminescent patches that absorb nearby light via a quantum biological phenomenon, creating zones of absolute darkness around them. Possibly 50,000+ cycles old each. They pre-date Osminoki evolution. They have never stopped growing.

**Hunting method:** Detection via EM, pressure, and chemical signatures. Approach from below. Generate a focused infrasound pulse (the Osminoki call it "the water screams"). Activate anti-bioluminescence so all nearby light dies. Extend tendrils to taste and assess prey. Create a suction vortex with feeding orifices. Swallow prey whole or in large pieces.

**Territory:** One inhabits each hemisphere (north and south), separated by an equatorial boundary. They never cross except for mating.

**Reproduction:** Hermaphroditic. Mate when conditions align (roughly every 8–15 cycles). Both migrate to the Equatorial Abyss (~22,000m), exchange genetic material simultaneously, return to their hemispheres. Both are now pregnant with two offspring each.

**Gestation (3–5 cycles):** Embryos develop in internal chamber, initially fed by nutrient secretions. At cycle 3–4, the offspring become parasitic, consuming the parent's internal organs while the parent continues hunting. The parent's body slowly hollows out. When ready, the parent descends to maximum depth, and the offspring erupt from within, 40–50 meters long, fully functional. The parent dies, allowing its children to consume the remains and gear up for a fight to the death for dominance.

**The Sibling Battle:** Both parents produce two offspring. The juveniles can sense each other and act upon it, actively hunting their twin. The survivor absorbs the other's mass and genetic memory, claims a hemisphere, and the cycle continues. **The species cannot exceed two mature individuals.** Self-fertilization is possible as an emergency measure if one dies without mating, but produces weaker young.

**What the Abbey Knows:** Almost nothing. Ancient Memory-Stones mention "the Twin Devourers." Only 3–4 direct encounters in 6,000+ cycles of recorded history, always at extreme depth. Survivors describe darkness, screaming water, tendrils, many mouths. The Abbey believes "the Light Eater" is a single ancient creature, possibly mythological. They don't know there are two, or anything about the reproductive cycle.
