# The Osminok System

## The Star: Osminok (K5V Orange Dwarf)

- **Mass:** 0.7 solar masses
- **Luminosity:** 0.3 solar luminosities
- **Age:** ~6 billion years
- **Peak output:** 600–700nm (orange-red), with less UV and more infrared than Sol
- **Character:** Stable, long-lived (20–40 billion year lifespan), dim orange glow

A K-type star provides the long evolutionary timeline needed for complex life while outputting less UV radiation. Its orange light filters through Osminok Prime's thick atmosphere to create a world of perpetual warm twilight tones.

## Osminok Prime

**Galactic Position:** Far outer rim, galactic halo region. Not on any major or minor trade routes, no known strategic resources, and its perpetual megastorms make it a navigation hazard. Not in most corporate star charts. Occasionally "rediscovered" by explorers or pirates. Word has gotten out in recent generations, drawing occasional trade ships, criminal elements seeking hiding spots, xenoanthropologists, and corporate labor recruiters, but Osminok is still largely ignored by the galactic mainstream.

**Physical Parameters:**

| Parameter | Value |
|:--|:--|
| **Radius** | 90% of Earth (~36,000 km circumference) |
| **Density** | 7.0 g/cm³ (iron-rich core) |
| **Surface Gravity** | 1.15× Earth |
| **Orbital Distance** | 0.55 AU |
| **Orbital Period** | ~180 Earth days (~1 cycle) |
| **Rotation** | 18-hour day |
| **Axial Tilt** | 35° |
| **Ocean Coverage** | 99%+ (quite wet) |
| **Surface Pressure** | 2.5 bar (2.5× Earth) |

**Atmosphere:** Thick, heavy, and highly reactive. Water vapor (~3–5% at sea level, increasing rapidly with altitude into the storm layers), sulfur dioxide from volcanic activity (~0.1–0.3%), traces of ammonia and methane. The combination of no continental landmasses, rapid rotation (strong Coriolis forces), massive tidal heating from Tsunari, and a powerful magnetic field driven by the iron-rich core creates the defining feature of Osminok Prime: **perpetual planetary megastorms**!

**The Megastorms:** Hurricane-force winds circle the entire planet without interruption. Storm systems merge and reinforce each other endlessly. Lightning is continuous, not occasional. The electromagnetic interference makes Osminok nearly invisible to standard orbital sensors. The storms vary in intensity with seasons and lunar phases but never fully stop.

"Clear" on Osminok means the megastorms have briefly weakened to merely "severe thunderstorm." This happens maybe 5–10 times per cycle, lasting anywhere from a few marbecs to (very rarely) a full malton unit.

**Ocean Depths:**

| Zone | Depth | Character |
|:--|:--|:--|
| Average depth | 6–8 km | Much deeper than Earth due to minimal continental shelf; settled |
| Common deep zones | 10–15 km | Trenches and abyssal canyons; well-traveled |
| Extreme depths | 18–22 km | Extreme geological activity; largely unexplored |
| The Unknowable Deep | 25+ km | Theoretical; Osminoki who attempt exploration never return |

**Ocean Chemistry:**

- Salinity: 3.8% (slightly above Earth's 3.5%)
- pH: 7.9–8.1 (slightly alkaline)
- Dissolved oxygen: 6–8 mg/L
- Higher CO2 and methane from geological/biological activity
- Hydrogen sulfide near hydrothermal vents

**Temperature Zones:**

- Surface (0–100m): 2–8°C
- Twilight (100–1,000m): 1–4°C
- Midnight (1,000–3,000m): 0–2°C
- Deep (3,000–7,000m): 1–3°C (warmer near vents)
- Abyss (7,000m+): 0–1°C (except near volcanic activity)

The ocean away from the poles doesn't freeze thanks to salinity lowering the freezing point to about -2°C, constant mixing from currents, geothermal input, and pressure effects. The perpetual cloud-layer albedo reflects the majority of the solar energy back to space before it reaches the ocean, acting as a planetary heat shield and keeping the waters chilly and well-oxygenated.

Photosynthesis is *not* the primary source of environmental O₂. The heavy lifting is done by chemosynthetic oxygen production from the planet's extensive vent-dwelling organisms, supplemented by constant electrolysis from continuous lightning strikes splitting atmospheric water vapor and dissolving the freed oxygen back into the ocean. The lightning contribution is dramatic and looks spectacular from orbit, but the quiet bacterial mats on the seafloor are what actually keep everything breathing. The iron-rich basaltic geology and alkaline output of the hydrothermal vents neutralizes sulfurous acid input from dissolved SO₂, keeping the ocean chemistry safely balanced.

### Osminok's Moon: Tsunari — Waving Sister
*"The Dead Sister Waving Through The Storms"*

> **Author's Note:** It's unclear if this means "waving to us" or "creating waves" — probably both.

**Physical Characteristics:**

| Parameter | Value |
|:--|:--|
| **Mass** | 0.3 Earth masses (25× Luna) |
| **Orbital Period** | ~8 malton units |
| **Orbital Distance** | ~180,000 km from Osminok Prime |
| **Composition** | Rocky/metallic, iron-nickel core |
| **Atmosphere** | None (lost billions of years ago) |
| **Magnetic Field** | None (core solidified ~2 billion years ago) |
| **Surface Temps** | -150°C to +100°C (no atmosphere to regulate) |

At this mass ratio (0.3:0.9), Tsunari was arguably once part of a **binary planet system** before losing significant mass. Its surface is heavily cratered and covered in dark basaltic plains from ancient lava flows, coated in pulverized regolith.

**The Scars:** Tsunari's surface has geometric patterns inconsistent with natural impacts:

- **The Lattice:** Parallel trenches across one hemisphere, perfectly spaced at 50 km intervals
- **The Craters That Aren't:** Circular depressions exactly 100 km in diameter, arranged in hexagonal patterns
- **The Vitrified Plains:** Regions where surface rock was flash-melted and cooled into glass
- **The Missing Mass:** Gravitational surveys indicate ~0.05 Earth masses of material has been removed

No known natural process accounts for this geometry. The scars pre-date the Osminoki by a wide margin; the last major surface disturbance dates to roughly 200,000–400,000 cycles ago, around when the megastorms began. What caused them remains an open question (see Cosmic Mysteries).

**Tides:** Tsunari drives tidal ranges of 40–100 meters in Osminok's open ocean (Earth's open-ocean range is 0.5–1m). This creates massive tidal currents the Osminoki use for navigation, regular nutrient upwelling, and islands that flood completely during high tide.

**Apparent Size in Sky:** About 6× the diameter of Luna. When visible through storm breaks, it dominates the sky at ~3 degrees arc. Always tinted copper-orange by the atmosphere.

### The Rings of Osminok
*"The Scattered Rings Of Bones That Smile Across Our Sky"*

**Composition:** 70% silicate rock, 20% metallic fragments, 10% water ice. The metallic fraction is unusually high for a natural ring system.

**Origin:** The bulk composition closely matches Tsunari's surface material, strongly suggesting the moon as the debris source. How this volume of material was ejected from a body of Tsunari's mass remains an open question; the event appears relatively recent on a geological timescale. Captured by Osminok Prime's gravity over millennia.

**Structure:** Primary ring system extends from 80,000–200,000 km from planet center, encompassing Tsunari's orbit. Multiple distinct bands of varying density and composition, with gaps cleared by Tsunari's gravity.

**Appearance from space:** Spectacular. Brilliant ice reflecting orange starlight, dark bands of rock, and glittering metallic fragments. From the surface during storm breaks, a thin silver-white arc stretching across the sky. Some metallic fragments catch starlight and glitter.

The Osminoki call the rings *"The Scattered Rings Of Bones That Smile Across Our Sky"* and do not refer to it with a shortname, implying a high degree of reverence. Legend says Tsunari was once whole and something broke it. This is technically true; they just don't know it was deliberate.

## Other Celestial Bodies

The Osminoki's understanding of their own system comes from three sources, each revealing progressively more.

**Storm-break observation** accounts for almost everything they knew before off-world contact: Tsunari, the rings, and two bright wanderers visible as colored points through gaps in the megastorms. They have no optical astronomy. There is no way to grind lenses underwater, no stable surface to mount them on, and nothing to point them at but raging static.

**Starsinger navigation data**, accumulated over thousands of cycles of FTL travel, fills in some gaps. The Singers sense gravitational wells and EM signatures during transit, and the Abbey has slowly assembled a rough chart of the system's major masses from flight records. This is how they know the wandering points of light are large, distant bodies rather than nearby small ones.

**Galactic survey data** provides everything else: detailed compositions, surface measurements, and the existence of dim outer bodies no Osminoki eye has ever seen. Most of what follows comes from this third source.

### Osminok I (0.25 AU) — Little Shadow
*"The Persistent Little Shadow That Chases The Light And Avoids Our Gaze"*

The innermost planet is a faint point the Osminoki occasionally glimpse near their star's orange glare during storm breaks, and that is about all they can tell you about it. Galactic survey data paints a harsher picture: a tidally locked rocky world of 0.6 Earth masses, its day side baked at 450°C, its night side frozen at -180°C, with no atmosphere to split the difference. Its surface is rich in heavy metals and marked by geometric scars matching those on Tsunari. Ancient automated mining infrastructure sits dormant across the sunlit hemisphere. Old corporate records list it as a "depleted resource zone," which may be why modern corporations haven't come looking.

### Osminok II (0.55 AU) — Ocean Home
*"The Endless Ocean That Is Our Treasured Home Despite Its Many Dangers"*

The homeworld. See [Osminok Prime](#osminok-prime) above. "Prime" is the galactic common name — the only planet in the system anyone has much reason to visit. This one has cute octopus people who will work for cheap!

### Osminok III (1.8 AU) — Red Wanderer
*"The Wanderer Of Red And Orange Whose Calls Still Echo In The Black"*

To the Osminoki, Osminok III is a rust-colored point of light that wanders slowly against the handful of stars visible during storm breaks. To galactic surveyors, it is a failed gas giant: 4 Earth masses of thick nitrogen-methane atmosphere pressing down at over 50 bar, tinted reddish-orange by iron oxide aerosols and methane haze. There is no solid surface beneath the clouds, only thickening gas that eventually becomes a supercritical fluid. It spins fast, one rotation every 11 hours, driving violent storm systems with an energy output that would make Osminok Prime's megastorms feel almost companionable. Nine moons orbit it: eight small captured asteroids and one larger icy body (0.1 Earth masses). Some of the smaller moons bear geometric surface scarring similar to Tsunari's.

The atmosphere composition is unusual for a world this massive. At 4 Earth masses and 1.8 AU, Osminok III should have captured a substantial hydrogen-helium envelope during formation. Instead, its upper atmosphere is almost entirely nitrogen and methane, with only trace hydrogen. The lighter gases that once formed the outermost layers are simply gone, as if something skimmed them off and left the heavier volatiles behind.

### Osminok IV (3.5 AU) — Blue Watcher
*"The Bright Blue Eye That Watches Us Fondly From A Great Distance"*

Ice giant, 14 Earth masses. Brilliant blue-white, its methane atmosphere absorbing red wavelengths and reflecting back a cold point of light that stands out sharply against Osminok's amber sky. Spectacular natural ice rings span 160,000 km: 95%+ pure water ice, formed roughly 100 million years ago when a moon crossed the Roche limit and was torn apart. 23 known moons, several with cryovolcanism. The brightest object in Osminok's night sky after Tsunari, and during the clearest storm breaks, its rings are sometimes barely detectable as a hazy line bisecting the point of light.

### Osminok V/Ossuary (8 AU) — Hidden Rock
*"The Cold Small Rock That Has Hidden From Us Always And Saddens Us"*

Galactic surveys designated this place "Ossuary" due to its coloration, which is quite similar to that of sun-bleached bones. A small, cold dwarf planet on a highly elliptical orbit, invisible from Osminok Prime's surface. 0.08 Earth masses. Known only from galactic survey data and corroborated by Starsinger flight records, which consistently register a gravitational anomaly at roughly this distance. The Osminoki named it after learning of its existence from off-world sources; the full name roughly translates to acknowledgment of something in their waters they had not yet touched. Its surface has not been closely examined.

### The Outer Dark (15–50+ AU) — Close Black
*"The Shelf Of Black That Is Closer Than The Black Beyond That Teuthida Touched"*

The system's cold outer reaches, far beyond anything the Osminoki can observe. The Fragment Field (15–20 AU) is an unusually dense metallic asteroid cluster, its composition heavy on processed alloys and refined metals in concentrations that don't occur naturally.

Galactic surveyors have noted that the Osminok system is unusually poor in accessible heavy elements for a system of its age and stellar class. The reasons for this are a matter of some interest (see Cosmic Mysteries).

### Osminok VI (45 AU) — Silent Stone
*"The Unturned Stone That Waits In Silence At The Edge Of The Shelf"*

A small, ancient, pristine world drifting at the far edge of the Outer Dark that appears to have never been touched by anything or anyone. The Osminoki, upon learning of its existence from off-world sources, named it and immediately began planning an expedition.

## What You Can See From the Surface

During those rare storm breaks (a few times per cycle), an observer on a craggy island might glimpse...

**The Sky:** Deep amber-orange, shifting from grey-black storm clouds to broken overcast. Everything is tinted copper by the K-star's light filtering through water vapor and sulfur compounds. Perpetual sunset quality; never true noon brightness.

**Tsunari:** Massive gleaming copper disc in the sky. Visible surface features: dark maria, lighter highlands, the biggest craters as dark spots, the Lattice as faint linear striations. During full phase, bright enough to see by even at shallow underwater depths. With an orbital period of only ~8 malton units, Tsunari cycles from new to full in about 4; an Osminoki who sees a crescent through one storm break may see a half-disc through the next, mere marbecs later. It is the most dynamic thing in their sky, and they know it intimately even through glimpses.

**The Rings:** Thin silver-white arc stretching across the sky, with occasional metallic glitters. Spectacular when backlit by a rising or setting full Tsunari.

**The Planets:** Osminok III as a bright rust-colored point. Osminok IV as a brilliant blue-white point, the brightest object after Tsunari. Osminok I rarely visible, close to the star's orange glare.

**The Stars:** Only a few dozen of the brightest visible through atmospheric haze. All orange-tinted. Heavy twinkling. No constellations (not enough stars visible). Most Osminoki have no concept of the true number of stars.

**The Auroras ("The Singer's Breath"):** Osminok's powerful magnetic field creates planet-wide auroras (not just polar). Orange, red, and purple curtains of light, visible even through moderate cloud cover and even from shallow underwater depths (200m) as a ghostly glow. Occurs 20–30 times per cycle (every few malton units) during active periods on the K-star.

**The Perfect Moment (once every few cycles):** Storm break + full Tsunari + nighttime + Osminok IV at opposition + aurora active. The copper moon, the silver ring, the blue eye, the purple curtains, all reflected in the churning ocean while Stormskates glide overhead. This is as close as an Osminoki can come to seeing what Teuthida saw when she first glimpsed the void. It's also incredibly dangerous, requiring standing in the hunting zone of surface predators.

Most Osminoki never witness this alignment. Those who do carry it as a defining memory. Gaby, for instance, saw it once as a juvenile on her pilgrimage to Threshold Crag, waiting 54 marbecs for the storm to break and the Stormskates to hunt elsewhere. She's never seen it again, but *gosh* does she want to.
