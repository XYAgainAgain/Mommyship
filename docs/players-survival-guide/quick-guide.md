# 5.0 QUICK GUIDE

|                       [**STAT CHECKS**](how-to-play.md#221-stat-checks)                        |                          [**SAVES**](how-to-play.md#222-saves)                           |                       [**PANIC CHECKS**](how-to-play.md#24-panic)                       |
| :----------------------------------------------------------: | :----------------------------------------------------------: | :----------------------------------------------------------: |
| Roll 1d100 under your Strength, Speed, Smarts, or Savvy to accomplish the task. If you fail, gain 1 Stress. A roll of 90–99 is always a failure; 00 is always a success. | Roll 1d100 under your Sanity, Fear, or Body to avoid danger, otherwise, fail and gain 1 Stress. A roll of 90–99 is always a failure; 00 is always a success. | When the worst has happened, to avoid Panicking, roll 1d20, aiming for greater than your character's current Stress. Rolling less than/equal to your Stress fails. Roll on [Panic Table](how-to-play.md#243-panic-table-d20). |
|                          [**SKILLS**](how-to-play.md#25-skills)                          |                  [**ADVANTAGE/DISADVANTAGE**](how-to-play.md#223-modifying-rolls)                  |                        [**CRITICALS**](how-to-play.md#221-stat-checks)                         |
| If a character has a Skill relevant to the task at hand, you can add its Skill Bonus to the Stat before rolling a Stat Check. **Trained Skills:** +10, **Expert Skills:** +15, **Master Skills:** +20 | If a character has a situational **Advantage [+]**, roll the dice twice and take the best result. If a character has a situational **Disadvantage [-]**, roll the dice twice and take the worst result. | Whenever you roll doubles on 1d100 (22, 77, etc.), it's Critical! On a Critical Success, something very good happens. On a Critical Failure, something bad happens, and you must make a Panic Check. |
|                   [**DAMAGE & WOUNDS**](how-to-play.md#264-damage)                    |                      [**ARMOR & COVER**](how-to-play.md#265-armor)                       |                          [**DEATH**](how-to-play.md#267-wounds-death)                           |
| Whenever a character takes Damage, reduce Health by that amount. If your Health is reduced to 0, gain 1 Wound and roll on the [Wounds Table](how-to-play.md#268-wound-effects-d10). | Ignore any Damage less than the AP of your Armor + Cover. Any Damage greater than that value of your AP reduces it by 1 and carries over. When it reaches 0 AP, the Armor or Cover is destroyed. | Whenever your character takes Wounds equal to their Maximum Wounds, make a Death Save. Roll 1d10 & cover it; only reveal the result when someone checks out your character's vitals. |
